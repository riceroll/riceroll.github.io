I am a final-year Ph.D. student in Morphing Matter Lab (recently moved to Berkeley), from Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), Human-Computer Interaction Institute (HCII), advised by Prof. Lining Yao, co-adviced by Prof. Ding Zhao.

My research focuses on computational design and fabrication for shape-morphing systems, with a focus on truss-based robotic structures and self-developing responsive materials . I develop novel mechanical and material mechanisms that enable precise, high-degree-of-freedom volumetric transformations while minimizing hardware complexity. I develop efficient algorithms and specialized models to address the design and control challenges that emerge from increasingly complex morphing mechanisms. My research interests lie at the intersection of geometries, robotic systems, and machine learning.

Prior to joining CMU, I received my bachelor’s degree from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China. Throughout my acadmic journey, I was fortunate to be mentored and collaborate with Prof. Alexandra Ion, Prof. Nikolas Martelaro, Prof. Guanyun Wang, Prof. Jim McCann, Prof. Chris Harrison, Prof. Teng Zhang, Prof. Levent Burak Kara, Prof. Jessica Yongjie Zhang.
